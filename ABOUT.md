# "ABOUT"

I'm a technologist and researcher working at the intersection of high-performance computing, data science, and signal analysis. Much of my work focuses on building scalable systems that transform complex data, particularly radio frequency signals, into meaningful insights. In many ways, this work is about turning invisible signals into something visible and interpretable.

Outside of my core technical work, I am interested in how computational thinking, systems design, and experimentation translate into creative disciplines. I am particularly drawn to frameworks that encourage remixing existing ideas, documenting process, and exploring the boundary between engineering and creative expression.

I am interested in the Archive Toolkit as a way to study and apply Virgil Abloh's methodology, especially his approach to iteration, cross-disciplinary thinking, and redefining how tools and systems can be used creatively.

Sarom Leang, "PhD."
